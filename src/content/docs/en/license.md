Dibao uses Business Source License 1.1 (BUSL-1.1) with an Additional Use Grant to implement a source-available, fair-code, delayed-open-source model. Each public release automatically converts to Apache License 2.0 after its own Change Date.

## What License Does Dibao Use?

The current version uses BUSL-1.1. The Change License is Apache-2.0.

## Is Dibao Fair-Code?

Yes. Dibao remains fair-code, source-available, and self-hostable. BUSL-1.1 is the legal mechanism for that model.

## Is It Open Source?

Before the Change Date, the current version is not open source in the OSI sense. After the Change Date, that specific release automatically becomes Apache-2.0.

## Can I Self-Host It?

Yes. Personal, family, research, evaluation, learning, and non-commercial use can self-host and use Dibao for free.

## Can A Company Use It Internally?

Yes. Companies, schools, nonprofits, and other organizations can use, modify, self-host, and run Dibao internally for free.

## Can I Charge A Client To Deploy It?

Yes, if the instance runs in the client's own environment, account, or controlled infrastructure. You may not turn Dibao into your own hosted platform, white-label product, resale product, or competing SaaS without a separate commercial license.

## Can I Offer Paid SaaS Or White Label?

No, unless you receive separate commercial authorization from the Licensor. Paid hosting, SaaS, managed service, cloud service, white-label, resale, and competing commercial products require commercial authorization. Contact: https://dibao.app.

## What Happens After Four Years?

That specific release automatically converts to Apache-2.0 at its Change Date.

## What About The Main Branch?

`main` is a development branch and may contain TODO dates or unreleased parameters. Release Date, Change Date, and the legal snapshot are frozen in each release tag's `LICENSE.md`.
